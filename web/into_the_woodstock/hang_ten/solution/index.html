First, fill in the rest of the letters to make odd phrases:

  • ALWAYS SAY LIVE
  • BIG GIRL BLUE
  • EGGS UNDER DIFFICULT
  • FALSE TO DEATH
  • GIVE THE SIT
  • HOT SOFT CASH
  • OLD CHILD LITERATURE
  • OUT THE PRIVATE EYE
  • WARM FOOT LUKE
  • WHITE AND PALE

The answers can all be made into familiar phrases by replacing two of their parts with their opposites. The reassembled phrases are:

  • NEVER SAY DIE
  • LITTLE BOY BLUE
  • EGGS OVER EASY
  • TRUE TO LIFE
  • TAKE THE STAND
  • COLD HARD CASH
  • YOUNG ADULT LITERATURE
  • IN THE PUBLIC EYE
  • COOL HAND LUKE
  • BLACK AND TAN

Sort by the given suffixes (which can be added to the second opposite words; STAND/ARD, PUBLIC/ATION, DIE/SIS, ADULT/ERY, HAND/SOME, HARD/WARE, BOY/ISH, TAN/DEM, LIFE/BOAT, EASY/GOING) to get:

  • TAKE THE STAND
  • IN THE PUBLIC EYE
  • NEVER SAY DIE
  • YOUNG ADULT LITERATURE
  • COOL-HAND LUKE
  • COLD HARD CASH
  • LITTLE BOY BLUE
  • BLACK AND TAN
  • TRUE TO LIFE
  • EGGS OVER EASY

and take the first letters to fill in the link, http://tiny.cc/lbte, which leads to a game on drawastickman.com. Playing the game to the end reveals the answer: CHRIST CHURCH REGATTA.
