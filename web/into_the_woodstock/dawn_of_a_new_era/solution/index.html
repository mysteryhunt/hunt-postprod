There are many approaches to arrive at the first aha, since many of the clues are attackable in different ways. The first aha is that each clue refers to an item on the flag of a country. Furthermore, each clued item is either a star or a horizontal stripe. The stars and horizontal stripes come in 7 colors. The clue list is ordered alphabetically by country to disambiguate some clues and to help you search quicker. Here are the clued items:

The next step is to notice that the numbers in parentheses are distinct and in the range 1-21. So you can reorder the list using the given numbers. After that, notice that the clued items of the same color are all consecutive in the new ordering, giving you 7 color groups.

Finally, the stars and horizontal stripes are meant to remind you of dots and dashes in morse code, a common puzzle encoding. Read one morse-encoded letter from each color group.
